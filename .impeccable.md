## Design Context

### Users
HEC Montréal finance students (MATH40602 – Quantitative Methods) learning to price structured products. They use the tool on laptops or large screens to build intuition about Monte Carlo simulation, payoff structures, and coupon mechanics before implementing in Matlab. The tool may be shown during class presentations.

### Brand Personality
Scholarly, precise, trustworthy.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Quantitative Textbook"** — like a beautifully typeset academic publication (Springer/Princeton University Press) brought to life as an interactive web tool. Edward Tufte meets Bloomberg Education.

- **Primary text**: Charcoal `#2d3436`
- **Secondary text**: Warm gray `#636e72`
- **Accent**: Deep teal `#00838f` (links, active states, key highlights)
- **Background**: Cream `#fafaf8`
- **Cards**: White `#ffffff` with very subtle shadow
- **Regime colors**: Muted green `#2e7d32`, muted amber `#e65100`, muted red `#c62828` (keep functional, not flashy)
- **Headings**: Serif font (Georgia or Palatino) for a scholarly feel
- **Body**: Clean sans-serif (system stack) for readability
- **Data/numbers**: Monospace (SF Mono, Consolas) for precision
- **Charts**: Muted, Tufte-inspired — minimal gridlines, no chartjunk, data-ink ratio maximized
- **Light mode only**

**Anti-references**: Bloomberg terminal dark UI, gamified fintech (Robinhood), overly corporate (PowerPoint blue). No gratuitous gradients, no rounded-everything, no emoji.

### Design Principles

1. **Data over decoration** — Every visual element should serve comprehension. Remove chartjunk, reduce non-data ink. If a border, shadow, or color doesn't aid understanding, drop it.

2. **Typography carries the hierarchy** — Use font size, weight, and serif/sans-serif contrast to create structure, not color blocks or heavy dividers. Headings in serif (Georgia), body in sans-serif, data in monospace.

3. **Teach through interaction** — Interactive elements (sliders, inputs, toggleable charts) should feel like turning pages in a well-designed textbook, not clicking buttons in a SaaS app. Transitions should be subtle (200ms, ease).

4. **Precision in presentation** — Numbers are formatted consistently (2 decimal places, proper signs, dollar signs). Tables are aligned. Formulas are rendered with KaTeX. Nothing looks approximate or sloppy.

5. **Breathing room** — Generous margins and padding. Content is never cramped. White space is a feature, not wasted space. Max-width container, comfortable line lengths (60-80 characters for prose).
